About us

Genomics for Community Dynamics and Evolution

Our Mission

GenCoDE is a curated platform for population genomic data structured at the scale of ecological communities. By utilizing untapped genomic variation within environmental and interaction contexts, GenCoDE enables predictive insights into community resilience, adaptation, and evolutionary change.

Our vision

We envision GenCoDE as a foundational resource for next-generation biodiversity science that supports:

  • Cross-taxa synthesis

  • Eco-evolutionary forecasting

  • Conservation decision-making

  • Open, reproducible genomic research

By curating and structuring genomic data at the scale of ecological communities, GenCoDE aims to unlock new insight into how biodiversity adapts, reorganizes, and persists in a changing world.

Who we are

GenCoDE is a collaborative effort led by faculty, postdoctoral researchers, and graduate students across disciplines, including:

  • Population and evolutionary genomics

  • Community ecology

  • Conservation biology

  • Quantitative biology and data science

We share a common goal: to aggregate population genomic datasets across taxonomic groups in order to investigate community-level genomic structure and dynamics.

Who we are

We are a group of faculty, postdoctoral researchers, graduate students at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, Carl R. Woese Institute for Genomic Biology.
