Our mission

GenCoDE is a curated platform for population genomic data structured at the scale of ecological communities. By utilizing untapped genomic variation within environmental and interaction contexts, GenCoDE enables predictive insights into community resilience, adaptation, and evolutionary change.

GenCoDE provides a structured gateway to studies spanning community genomics, population structure, demographic inference, stochastic modeling, and statistical learning. This resource highlights both foundational theory and emerging analytical approaches, offering researchers a coherent entry point into a rapidly converging field.

Our database

The GenCoDE database is designed around a simple premise: genomic variation becomes most informative when interpreted alongside community composition, species interactions, and environmental variability.

  • 5,120 Genes
  • 8,192 SNPs
  • 2,048 Species
  • 4,096 Taxa
  • 1,024 Communities

Our platform aggregates population genomic datasets collected across diverse taxonomic groups — from plants and microbes to animals — while preserving links to spatial, temporal, and ecological metadata. Rather than functioning as a raw data repository, GenCoDE serves as a community-level information layer, providing standardized summary statistics, cross-community descriptors, and clear pathways for connecting with contributing research groups to access underlying datasets. Initial efforts emphasize regions characterized by exceptional biodiversity and established monitoring networks. These locations provide critical benchmarks for evaluating theoretical predictions and simulation-based models. By combining legacy datasets with coordinated new sampling, GenCoDE enables analyses that span multiple spatial scales, taxonomic groups, and ecological conditions.
