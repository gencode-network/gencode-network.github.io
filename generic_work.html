Developing genomic data and ecological models into real conservation action and strategies requires an accurate understanding of the relationship between genomic metrics and ecological and evolutionary characteristics. GenCoDe researchers are currently working on using community genomic data and simulations to investigate how environmental interactions and histories shape patterns recorded in the genomes of organisms and how current genetic information tracks community population dynamics over time. With a knowledge of how environmental stochasticity and demography influence genomic data, we can validate and map these relationships, using genetics to infer ancestry information we do not otherwise have access to, and use this insight to better inform strategies that protect biodiversity and ecological functioning.

Who we are

We are a group of faculty, postdoctoral researchers, graduate students at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, Carl R. Woese Institute for Genomic Biology.
